Apple

Apple Inc., founded by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne in 1976, began as a computer company in a garage in Cupertino, California.
The release of the Apple I and later the Apple II revolutionized personal computing, solidifying Apple's place in the industry.
However, it faced challenges in the 1980s with the departure of Jobs and increased competition.
Jobs returned in 1997 and led Apple's resurgence with iconic products like the iMac, iPod,
iPhone, and iPad, transforming it into one of the most valuable and influential technology companies globally.
With a focus on design, innovation, and user experience, Apple continues to push boundaries in technology,
software, and services, maintaining its position as a leader in the industry.
